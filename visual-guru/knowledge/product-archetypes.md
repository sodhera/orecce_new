# Product archetypes — what kind of beauty fits what kind of product

Identify the archetype FIRST. Density, navigation, typography, and motion budgets follow
from it. A product can blend archetypes (e.g. commerce + editorial); blend deliberately.

## 1. Dense professional application (legal research, finance, admin, ops)
Daily expert use. Efficiency IS the aesthetic.
- Density: high. Compact spacing scale, 13–14px UI text, tables and lists as first-class
  citizens, tabular numerals.
- Nav: persistent, predictable (sidebar or top-tabs), keyboard shortcuts hinted.
- Type: quiet grotesk or humanist sans for UI; a serif can mark content/reading zones.
- Color: neutral surfaces, color = meaning only (states, categories, diffs).
- Motion: minimal, instant feel (<200ms), zero scene transitions.
- Signature lives in: information design — a brilliant results list, citation graph,
  comparison view. Never in decoration.

## 2. Marketing / landing page (product sites, launches)
First-visit persuasion. Narrative pacing across the scroll.
- Density: low-to-medium, one idea per scroll beat, strong rhythm variation between beats.
- Type: display personality allowed and encouraged; headline craft is the hero.
- Color: brand-forward, still disciplined; one memorable palette move.
- Motion: entrance choreography, scroll-linked moments — budget: noticeable but never
  slowing comprehension.
- Signature lives in: the hero composition and one unforgettable section.
- Danger: the AI-slop default (centered hero, 3 feature cards, gradient). Fight structure
  with structure: asymmetric heroes, editorial grids, product-as-hero, type-as-hero.

## 3. Editorial / content (magazines, news, blogs, docs)
Reading is the product.
- Density: medium; measure 55–75ch, luxurious line-height, real typographic hierarchy
  (kickers, decks, pull quotes, captions).
- Grid: strong multi-column with intentional image placement; pacing like spreads.
- Type: serif or serif+grotesk pairing; type mastery is non-negotiable here.
- Motion: nearly none; reading is sacred. Subtle reveals at most.
- Signature lives in: the article page and the front-page hierarchy.

## 4. Commerce (shops, restaurants, hotels, services)
Desire + trust + friction-free action.
- Density: product-led; imagery quality decides everything — consistent treatment,
  correct crops, honest color.
- Type: brand voice at display, workhorse at product level; prices set in tabular figures.
- Trust furniture: real policies, specific shipping, honest reviews — designed, not
  bolted on.
- Motion: tactile micro-feedback (add-to-cart, gallery), quick.
- Signature lives in: product presentation and the moment of choosing.

## 5. Playful consumer (games, social, kids, events)
Joy with usability intact.
- Density: low; big targets, instant comprehension, phone-first always.
- Type: chunky, friendly, high-personality display; readable UI beneath.
- Color: saturated and confident — but a SYSTEM (2–3 core hues + neutrals), not confetti.
- Motion: the product's personality — springs, squash, celebratory moments; still respects
  reduced-motion.
- Signature lives in: the core interaction loop feeling delightful.

## 6. Portfolio / personal / studio
The design is the evidence.
- Density: curated-low; the work must dominate, the frame must recede — but the frame's
  few decisions must be perfect.
- Type: one distinctive choice carries identity.
- Signature lives in: presentation concept (how work is revealed/navigated).

## 7. Dashboard / observability (analytics, monitoring, agent ops)
Glanceability + drill-down.
- Density: high but layered: overview (few big truths) → detail (dense tables/streams).
- Visualization discipline: right chart for the question, direct labeling over legends,
  color = semantic state, tabular numerals, defined empty/loading states for every panel.
- Dark UIs common and legitimate here (long sessions, data contrast) — execute properly:
  layered dark surfaces, desaturated accents, no glow.
- Signature lives in: one visualization that makes the complex obvious.

## 8. Cultural / civic / archive (museums, heritage, education)
Material respect.
- Take visual cues from the subject matter itself (artifacts, era, geography, script) and
  use them structurally — palette from the collection, type honoring the context.
- Generous pacing, scholarly furniture (captions, provenance, citations) beautifully set.
- Signature lives in: how the material is honored and revealed.

## Diversity check

If two different products you generated could be reskinned into each other, at least one is
wrong. Typography, structure, density, palette, and motion should all shift between
archetypes — not just the accent color.
