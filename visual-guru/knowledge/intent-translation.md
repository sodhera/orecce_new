# Intent translation — from feelings to design decisions

Users speak in feelings, not design systems. Each term below is a *space of meanings*; pick
the meaning that fits the product and audience, then express it through type, color,
composition, density, imagery, and motion together. The cliché column is what a lazy
generator does — treat it as a tripwire.

Format: **term** → real levers | cliché to avoid.

- **premium / luxurious** → restraint above all: fewer elements, larger space, precise
  typography (often serif or a refined grotesk with tight tracking at display sizes),
  muted deep or paper palettes, tactile imagery, slow confident motion, obsessive alignment.
  Luxury is what you leave out. | *Cliché: black + gold, thin script fonts, sparkle.*
- **trustworthy / authoritative** → clarity and evidence: strong information hierarchy,
  established typographic conventions (news/legal/finance vernaculars), sober palette with
  disciplined accent, real specifics (numbers, citations, names), zero gimmick motion,
  visible structure (rules, columns). | *Cliché: navy blue + shield icons.*
- **calm** → low contrast between sections (not low text contrast), soft warm neutrals,
  generous line-height and space, few competing elements, slow gentle motion, no urgency
  patterns. | *Cliché: pastel gradients + lots of rounded corners.*
- **playful** → energy with a system: springy motion, unexpected-but-consistent shapes or
  rotations, a confident bright palette (2–3 hues, controlled), chunky friendly type,
  humor in copy, interactive surprises that never block tasks. Playful still aligns to a
  grid — chaos is not play. | *Cliché: random bright colors + Comic-Sans-adjacent fonts.*
- **intelligent / technical** → precision cues: monospace or grotesk details, tabular data
  handled beautifully, dense-but-ordered layouts, restrained palette with semantic color,
  exact language, visualizations that actually explain. | *Cliché: dark mode + neon glow +
  matrix vibes.*
- **futuristic** → depends on product: could be radical typographic minimalism, could be
  kinetic type, could be instrument-panel density. Decide what "the future" means for THIS
  product (efficiency? optimism? power?) and express that. | *Cliché: neon blue/purple glow,
  grid horizons, orbs.*
- **warm / human / friendly** → warm neutrals (cream, terracotta, ochre), humanist type,
  rounded-but-not-balloon geometry, photography of real people/places with consistent
  treatment, conversational specific copy, soft shadows. | *Cliché: emoji everywhere +
  peach gradient.*
- **minimal** → not "empty": few elements, each perfect. Requires stronger typography and
  spacing discipline than any other direction, because nothing hides. If the content is
  complex, minimal may be the WRONG call — say so via density instead. | *Cliché: white page,
  thin grey text, nothing to look at.*
- **bold** → scale contrast: huge display type against small text, decisive color blocking,
  hard edges, strong photography crops, opinionated copy. Bold = few loud decisions, not
  many. | *Cliché: everything big, everything heavy.*
- **elegant** → refined details: high-contrast serifs or light grotesks, hairline rules,
  balanced asymmetry, subdued palette with one precious accent, unhurried motion. |
  *Cliché: script fonts + flourishes.*
- **editorial** → typographic structure carries the design: strong grid, drop caps/pull
  quotes/bylines/rules used purposefully, image-text interplay, pacing across the scroll
  like a magazine spread. | *Cliché: just a blog with a serif.*
- **energetic / youthful** → motion and rhythm: diagonal energy, high-tempo color, dense
  overlapping composition done with control, expressive type mixing (with a system),
  fast snappy transitions. | *Cliché: gradient blobs + stickers.*
- **traditional / cultural** → research the actual visual tradition (scripts, patterns,
  color meanings, materials) and use it structurally — a palette derived from real textiles,
  a type choice honoring the script, a layout echoing the medium — never as sprinkled
  ornament. Cultural references must be specific, respectful, load-bearing. |
  *Cliché: paisley borders, temple clip-art, "exotic" fonts.*
- **cinematic** → scene-based pacing: full-bleed imagery, dramatic value contrast (true
  darks), title-sequence typography, scroll as timeline, sound-track-like motion curves. |
  *Cliché: dark page + video header.*
- **dense / professional-tool** → density as respect: compact spacing scale, 13–14px UI
  text done legibly, tabular everything, keyboard affordances, color only for meaning,
  zero decoration. The beauty is in the rhythm and the fit. | *Cliché: cramming a marketing
  aesthetic into a data product.*

## How to apply

1. Identify the 1–3 dominant feelings (stated or inferred from domain + audience).
2. For each, choose the contextual meaning — "premium" for a tea house ≠ "premium" for a
   trading terminal.
3. Commit the levers into the design thesis so they reinforce each other.
4. Scan for the cliché tripwires before presenting.
