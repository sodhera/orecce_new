Why a tea house, not a brand

We don't own the gardens. We know them.

Kyanjin Tea House began as an actual tea house — four tables beside the trail, run by our grandmother Nyima. Trekkers kept asking to take the tea home. Forty years later we still work the same way: the growers set their prices, we pay before harvest, and every packet names the person who picked it.

After the 2015 earthquake we rebuilt with the village, and one euro from every packet still goes to the Langtang school rebuild fund. That is not a marketing line; the receipts are published every December.

— Tenzin & Pema Lamathird generation, Kyanjin Gompa

Shipping

Weekly dispatch from Kathmandu every Monday. EU & UK 6–9 days (€6, free over €60); North America 8–12 days (€9); elsewhere by quote. Every parcel is trackable.

Freshness

We sell one harvest per year and print the picking dates on every packet. When a lot runs out, it's gone until next spring — we will not blend to stretch it.

Brewing

Each packet carries the grower's own brewing note. As a rule: more leaf, cooler water, shorter steeps — high-altitude leaf is generous and forgives nothing.
