Welcome from the Gurung family

A lodge with its boots by the door.

Naurikot began when our parents, Pema and Tsering, opened two spare rooms to walkers in 1996. The house has grown, but the rhythm has not: tea when you arrive, dinner around the stove, and honest advice about the trail ahead.

We are in the old village, ten minutes uphill from Manang’s main street. It is quieter here. Every room faces south; blankets are thick, showers are solar-heated, and our kitchen serves dal bhat, buckwheat pancakes, thukpa, and apple pie from Braga fruit.

— Sonam, Lhakpa & family

The road to Manang

Getting here is part of the journey.

Allow time to acclimatize.
The safest itinerary sleeps in Pisang before Manang. If you feel unwell, descend—never push higher to keep a booking.

01 · 760 m

Kathmandu

Morning bus or private jeep to Besisahar. Allow 6–8 hours depending on traffic.

02 · 760 m

Besisahar

Change to a shared local jeep. Most leave before 2 pm; book a seat the evening prior.

03 · 3,200 m

Upper Pisang

Sleep here. Walk the high route via Ghyaru for views, or take the gentler valley trail.

04 · 3,540 m

Naurikot

From Manang gate, follow the stone lane uphill past the old monastery for ten minutes.
