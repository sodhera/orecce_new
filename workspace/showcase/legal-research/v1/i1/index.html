Kumari Thapa v. Bhairab Bahadur Rana

Citation NKP 2076-61-9, Dec. 10312 Decided 2076-05-28 BS (2019-09-14 AD) Bench Rana CJ, Shrestha, Karki, Bhattarai, Tumbahang JJ
Held

Continuous and hostile possession of registered land, however long, does not extinguish registered title unless the possessor proves the register entry itself was obtained by fraud or material error. Equitable protection of possession operates against ejectment procedure, not against title.

14The petitioner's learned counsel invited us to follow the line of authority beginning with Ram Prasad Bishta v. Gauri Kumari Devi (NKP 2049), in which long possession coupled with payment of land revenue was held to raise an equity enforceable against the registered owner. That line of authority, developed under the former Muluki Ain, cannot survive the scheme of registration introduced by the Land (Survey & Measurement) Act, 2019 and carried into the Civil Code, 2074.

15Registration is not a mere fiscal record. Section 6 confers presumptive title, and the presumption is displaced only by proof that the entry was procured by fraud, coercion, or material error of survey — not by the register's silence in the face of possession. To hold otherwise would make every registered title provisional upon vigilance, and reward the trespasser's patience over the owner's paper.

16We are conscious that in the hills, and among families divided by migration, possession and paper often part ways for a generation. The answer to that hardship is the limitation on ejectment procedure in Maya Devi Shrestha (NKP 2068), not the quiet transfer of title by the passage of time.
